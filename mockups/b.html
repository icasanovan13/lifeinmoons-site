Life in Moons

One thousand
moons.

That is a long life — every full moon of it on one screen. Keep scrolling; watch it fill.

Not in days, which slip by uncounted, or years, which are too large to feel. In full moons — one of the oldest measures there is.

Every person who has ever lived looked up at the same moon you’ll see tonight. It is the one clock we have always shared.

The moons behind you, lit. The moon above you now, breathing. The ones still ahead, waiting as faint outlines.

Each one will hold whatever you give it — a note, a photograph, a milestone worth remembering — or stay empty forever. Together they become a quiet record of a life.
